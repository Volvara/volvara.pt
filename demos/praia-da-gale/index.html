Our story

A small door opened on the sea in June 1982.

José Costa Brás and Maria Isabel Marta Grade opened a modest room overlooking the Galé sand. Forty-four years on, the same family still sets the table — a little larger, the same Atlantic.

1982
The first door

A narrow seafront room, a single dining hall, and a promise: fresh fish from our own coastline, nothing that wouldn't be at home on our family table.

2000s
Room to grow

The terrace was rebuilt, the cold display added, the aquarium installed. What began as a summer room became a destination — for families from Britain, Germany, France and back home in Portugal.

Today
The same table

Open every day, ten until eleven. Same family on the pass, same view from the window, same Atlantic brought in at dawn. Some things are worth not changing.

What we cook

Three sources. One coast.

α

Wild Atlantic fish

Sea bass, wreckfish, sardines in season, monkfish from the local fleet. Chosen at dawn from the day's catch. On ice in the display — point to what you want, it comes grilled with olive oil, salt, lemon.

From our coast · daily
β

Ria Formosa shellfish

The lagoon sixty kilometres east is among Europe's finest bivalve beds. Clams, razorclams, oysters — traceable, alive from the tank, served the most traditional way we know.

Ria Formosa · PDO
γ

Selected meat

Black Angus sirloin, corn-finished over 200 days. Iberian pork shoulder from hand-picked animals. Lamb cutlets with mint, because some classics earn their keep.
